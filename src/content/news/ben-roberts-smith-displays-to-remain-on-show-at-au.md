The Australian War Memorial has confirmed that the displays dedicated to Victoria Cross recipient Ben Roberts-Smith will remain on public exhibition in Canberra, though they now feature a significant addition. A newly installed explanatory panel has been placed alongside the exhibit, providing visitors with context regarding the recent legal developments and judicial findings concerning the former soldier’s conduct during his service in Afghanistan.

This decision follows a protracted period of public debate and the conclusion of a high-profile civil defamation suit in the Federal Court. The institution, which serves as the heart of Australia’s military commemoration, has faced mounting pressure to address the gravity of the court's findings while fulfilling its mandate to document the nation's military history. The updated display aims to offer a more comprehensive narrative, acknowledging both the decorations awarded for bravery and the subsequent legal scrutiny that has followed.

> "The challenge for the Memorial lies in maintaining the sanctity of our military honours while courageously confronting the uncomfortable complexities of historical truth and judicial accountability."

Memorial officials have articulated that their primary responsibility is to the historical record, ensuring that the evolution of an individual's service history is transparently communicated to the public. By retaining the display but adding the new text, the AWM seeks to balance the recognition of gallantry with the weight of the civil court’s determinations. This move is seen as a pivotal step in how Australia navigates the intersection of contemporary legal outcomes and its tradition of wartime remembrance.
