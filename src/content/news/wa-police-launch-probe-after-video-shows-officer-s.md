Western Australia Police have launched a high-level internal investigation following the circulation of video footage depicting an officer using physical force against a woman during an arrest in the state’s far north. The Professional Standards Division was alerted after the mobile phone recording, which appears to show the officer shoving the individual to the ground, began circulating across social media platforms over the weekend.

Regional commanders have confirmed that the incident occurred in a remote Kimberley community and that all available evidence, including body-worn camera footage and witness testimonies, is currently under review. While the circumstances preceding the arrest remain part of the active inquiry, the vision has already ignited a broader conversation regarding the proportionality of force used by frontline responders in regional jurisdictions.

> "When the line between necessary restraint and excessive force is blurred, the fundamental trust between the constabulary and the community is placed under immediate and severe duress."

The outcome of this probe is expected to address whether standard operating procedures were breached during the encounter. As the investigation continues, WA Police maintain that they hold their officers to the highest standards of accountability, ensuring that any actions taken in the line of duty are consistent with both legislative requirements and community expectations.
