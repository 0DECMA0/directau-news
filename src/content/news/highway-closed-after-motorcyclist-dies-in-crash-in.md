A major arterial route in southern New South Wales has been shuttered following a fatal motorcycle collision, marking the state's first road fatality of the Easter long weekend. NSW Police and emergency crews responded to the scene in the state's south early this morning, where the rider was tragically pronounced deceased.

The incident has caused widespread logistical challenges for holiday travellers, with mandatory detours through regional terrain expected to add up to five hours to journey times. Crash investigation units remain on-site as authorities work to establish the circumstances surrounding the accident and clear the thoroughfare for safe passage.

> "The commencement of the holiday period serves as a grim testament to the fragility of life on our regional corridors, where a single moment can transform a family journey into a profound community tragedy."

Transport for NSW is advising motorists to exercise extreme caution and maintain patience while the highway remains closed. Travellers are encouraged to monitor live updates and factor significant delays into their trip planning as the investigation continues into the afternoon.
