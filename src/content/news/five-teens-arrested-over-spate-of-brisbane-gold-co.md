Queensland Police have taken five teenagers into custody following a violent string of home invasions spanning from South Brisbane to the Gold Coast overnight. The arrests follow a series of break-and-enter offences that left several residents with physical injuries and communities on high alert as authorities piece together the timeline of the alleged crime spree.

Investigations suggest the group targeted multiple residential properties in rapid succession, marking a significant escalation in youth-related property crime. Forensic teams remain on-site at several locations today, while local area commands have increased patrols in the affected suburbs to provide reassurance to the public following what has been described as a highly distressing series of events.

> "This latest wave of aggression underscores a disturbing trend where residential sanctuary is increasingly compromised, forcing a necessary re-evaluation of current deterrents and community safety protocols."

The five suspects are currently assisting police with their inquiries, with charges expected to be laid later this afternoon. As the judicial process begins, the focus remains on the recovery of the victims and the broader implications for youth justice policy within the Sunshine State.
