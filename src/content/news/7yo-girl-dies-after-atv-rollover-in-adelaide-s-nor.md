A seven-year-old girl has tragically lost her life following an all-terrain vehicle (ATV) rollover in Adelaide’s north-eastern outskirts on Easter Saturday. Emergency services were called to the private property in response to the accident, but despite the exhaustive efforts of paramedics, the young child could not be saved.

The incident marks a somber start to the holiday period for the South Australian community, casting a shadow over the festive break. This loss adds to a devastating weekend on the state's roads and rural properties, occurring shortly after a separate incident in the Adelaide Hills where two motorcyclists also lost their lives in a collision.

> "The fragility of life is never more apparent than during our periods of rest, reminding us that safety remains a non-negotiable priority across every terrain."

Local authorities have launched a full investigation into the circumstances surrounding the rollover to determine the exact cause of the tragedy. As families across the nation observe the Easter long weekend, South Australia Police continue to urge extreme caution for those operating recreational vehicles and travelling on regional roads.
