Police have launched an extensive manhunt following a brazen daylight heist at a local service station where an unidentified offender absconded with approximately $3,000 worth of diesel. The incident, which escalated rapidly from a commercial theft to a life-threatening confrontation, has left authorities concerned over the increasing audacity of fuel-related crimes across the region.

The situation turned critical when a service station attendant, having identified the ongoing theft, attempted to intercept the suspect on the forecourt. In a desperate bid to escape, the driver allegedly accelerated directly towards the staff member, narrowly avoiding a collision that could have resulted in a catastrophic physical outcome. Detectives are currently scrutinising high-resolution CCTV footage to identify the vehicle and the individual involved.

> "This incident underscores a volatile shift in criminal behaviour, where the economic desperation of high-value theft frequently gives way to a reckless disregard for human life."

Local law enforcement has issued a stern reminder to business operators to prioritse personal safety over the recovery of assets, urging staff not to engage with suspects in high-stakes scenarios. As the search for the driver continues, investigators are calling for anyone with dashcam footage from the vicinity at the time of the incident to come forward immediately.
