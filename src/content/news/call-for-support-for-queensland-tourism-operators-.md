Tourism operators in Far North Queensland are sounding the alarm as the region’s peak winter season faces an unprecedented wave of cancellations, attributed primarily to escalating fuel prices and supply volatility.

Regional caravan park managers have reported a stark shift in consumer behaviour, with the volume of travellers withdrawing bookings now surpassing new enquiries. This trend threatens to undermine the economic stability of the 'core months,' a period traditionally relied upon to sustain local businesses throughout the calendar year.

> "The tyranny of distance, once a charm of the Australian outback, has become a financial barrier that threatens to isolate our most vulnerable regional economies."

As the industry grapples with these logistical hurdles, stakeholders are urging state and federal authorities to implement targeted support measures. The objective remains to safeguard the livelihoods of those anchored in the northern tourism corridor before the seasonal window closes entirely.
