A local woman’s attempt to foster community spirit has ended in a costly legal headache after she was slugged with a $6,000 fine by the local council. Peita Gardiman organised what she described as a 'tiny' swap meet, intended to provide a much-needed boost to a struggling local business. However, the well-meaning gesture quickly soured when council compliance officers intervened, citing a lack of official permits for the gathering.

The dispute highlights the growing tension between grassroots community initiatives and strict local government regulations. While Gardiman maintains the event was a small-scale meet-up with minimal impact, the council insists that all public events, regardless of their size or intent, must adhere to rigorous safety and planning protocols. For Gardiman, the hefty penalty represents a disproportionate response to an act of kindness in a challenging economic climate.

The incident has sparked a wave of support for Gardiman on social media, with many locals questioning the council’s heavy-handed approach to community-led events. As the $6,000 fine looms, the case serves as a cautionary tale for any Aussie looking to help out their neighbourhood without first navigating the mountain of red tape required by local authorities. DirectAU.news has reached out to the council for further comment on the enforcement action.
