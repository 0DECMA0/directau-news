Victoria Police have arrested three 14-year-old boys following a distressing incident in Geelong that has sparked widespread community concern. Investigators took the youths into custody on Tuesday after footage surfaced depicting a group of five individuals allegedly engaging in a sustained physical confrontation against another teenager. The incident, which was captured on video and widely circulated, shows the victim being subjected to multiple strikes and kicks while on the ground.

The legal proceedings come as authorities continue to search for two other suspects believed to be involved in the daylight altercation. Local police have described the nature of the event as particularly egregious, noting that the recorded vision played a critical role in identifying the primary offenders. While the victim's physical injuries are being monitored, the psychological impact of such a public ordeal remains a priority for support services in the regional city.

> "This surge in documented youth-led altercations signals a troubling shift where the recording of harm is valued as much as the act itself, challenging our traditional frameworks of community policing and social responsibility."

As the three teenagers await their first court appearance, the incident has reignited a debate regarding youth justice and street safety in regional Victoria. Police maintain that while the arrests mark a significant step in the investigation, they are urging any further witnesses or those with dashcam footage from the Geelong area to come forward. The remaining suspects are encouraged to present themselves at a local station as inquiries continue into the circumstances surrounding the unprovoked event.
