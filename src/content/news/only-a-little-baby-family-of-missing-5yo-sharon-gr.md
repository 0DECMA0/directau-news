The family of five-year-old Sharon Granites has issued a heart-wrenching public appeal from Alice Springs, marking the first time they have spoken since the young girl vanished. Described as an "energetic" child, her relatives have urged the public to provide any information that might lead to her safe return, reminding the community that she remains "only a little baby" in need of protection.

Northern Territory Police have maintained a significant presence in the region, conducting thorough sweeps as the search for the missing youngster enters a high-stakes window. The family's decision to speak with the ABC underscores the gravity of the situation, as they seek to keep Sharon’s face and story at the forefront of the public consciousness during these critical hours.

> "The disappearance of a child in the Red Centre is not merely a local tragedy, but a profound test of the social fabric that binds our remote communities together."

Authorities are calling on residents and travellers to review any available dashcam or CCTV footage recorded in the Alice Springs area over the past forty-eight hours. Anyone with information, no matter how seemingly insignificant, is encouraged to contact local police or Crime Stoppers immediately to assist in the ongoing efforts to bring Sharon home to her loved ones.
