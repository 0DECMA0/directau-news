Liquidators have uncovered a staggering financial fallout following the collapse of Queensland Indoor Arenas, revealing that the company owes significant sums to creditors beyond an existing $61 million tax debt. The sports centre operator, which managed major facilities across Bundaberg, Brisbane, and Logan, has officially entered liquidation as the true extent of its liabilities becomes clear to investigators.

A total of 140 secured and unsecured creditors have been identified in the fallout, including a mix of local suppliers and former employees left out of pocket. Managed by Colin and Ashlee Kinnest, the company’s sudden downfall has sent shockwaves through the regional sporting community, with many staff members now facing deep uncertainty regarding their unpaid entitlements and superannuation.

The liquidators are continuing their forensic investigation into the company’s accounts to determine if any assets can be recovered for those affected. With the massive debt to the Australian Taxation Office taking priority, the addition of substantial private debt highlights the severity of the financial crisis that has brought the multi-city operation to a standstill.
